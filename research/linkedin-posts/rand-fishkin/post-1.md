Post Content:
Two big changes in marketing thanks to the new world of Zero Click Everything:

1) SEO is about influencing people in their search results, not getting clicks and traffic.
2) CRO is about optimizing brand perception and purchase decisions BEFORE customers ever reach your website.

Core argument:
In a zero-click discovery environment, the persuasive moment is shifting away from owned websites and into external channels like social feeds, communities, and AI/search result interfaces.

Implication for teams:
- SEO success should be measured by impact on sales and brand demand, not only traffic volume.
- CRO now extends upstream into pre-click experiences where buyers form opinions.
- Visibility without click-through can still be commercially valuable if it shapes perception and intent.

Closing idea:
SEO and CRO are not disappearing, but both functions need new success metrics tied to influence, trust, and revenue outcomes.
