Title: If I Started LinkedIn in 2026, I'd Do This
Source: YouTube (speaker: Pierre Herubel)
Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4OVxO2j24sw

Cleaned Transcript:

I got my first 10,000 LinkedIn followers in 2022. Today I have over 157,000 followers and more than 50 million impressions.

If I had to start again from zero, this is exactly how I would go from 0 to 10,000 followers.

The process has three phases:
- Phase 1: 0 to 2,000 followers
- Phase 2: 2,000 to 5,000 followers
- Phase 3: 5,000 to 10,000 followers

Each phase has a specific goal.

## Phase 1 (0 to 2,000): Build foundations

At this stage, your goal is not virality. Your goal is clarity, consistency, and credibility.

### Step 1: Choose one clear focus

Use this rule:
- One audience
- One problem
- One offer

If you speak to everyone, you speak to no one.

Example:
"I help B2B founders generate inbound leads through content."

It can be even more specific, but it must be clear and focused.

### Step 2: Turn your profile into a landing page

Your profile must convert visits into follows.

Do this:
- Add a clear banner with your value proposition
- Write a headline that says what you do, who you help, and the result
- Use the featured section to show proof of work or a guide
- Add a simple CTA (for example: "Follow me for daily marketing insights")

Your content should create curiosity, and your profile should close the follow decision.

### Step 3: Publish 30 days of authority content

Choose three core topics linked to your expertise.

Post useful insights daily using frameworks like:
- Problem
- Agitate
- Solution

Engagement may be low at first. That is normal. The goal is consistency and learning.

### Step 4: Engage 10x more than you post

At the beginning, comments are a growth lever.

Leave smart comments (not generic comments) on relevant creators' posts.

Benefits:
- More impressions than posting in silence
- More profile visits
- Faster recognition in your niche

By 2,000 followers, you should have:
- Clear positioning
- A converting profile
- 30+ days of authority content
- A small network built through comments and DMs

## Phase 2 (2,000 to 5,000): Build authority

Now your mission is to become known for something specific in a wider network.

### Step 1: Double down on what already works

Review your last 30-60 posts and ask:
"What topics, hooks, and formats got traction?"

Then create new versions of those winning ideas.

Build small content series around proven patterns.

### Step 2: Start publishing infographics

Use simple visual summaries to increase dwell time, saves, shares, and reposts.

Guidelines:
- One clear idea per infographic
- Clean visuals
- Short sentences
- Practical value

### Step 3: Share unique lived-experience insights

Stop repeating generic advice.

Share:
- What you actually did
- The process you used
- The results you got
- The problems you encountered

People want real operator insights, not recycled theory.

### Step 4: Build your tribe strategically

Treat LinkedIn like a networking event.

Map the key people in your niche:
- Creators
- Founders
- Operators
- Journalists

Engage, connect, DM, and build relationships with intention.

By 5,000 followers, your audience should:
- Recognize your name
- Trust your content
- Associate you with a clear domain

## Phase 3 (5,000 to 10,000): Build compounding systems

Most people fail here because this phase is long and requires systems.

### Step 1: Build a content system (not post-by-post output)

Use the "1 to 15" mindset:
- One strong insight
- Repurposed into many formats (text post, carousel, infographic, short video)

Use the 30/70 repurposing rule:
- 30% time on new ideas
- 70% time on proven ideas

This keeps quality and consistency high without constant reinvention.

### Step 2: Multiply formats while keeping one message

Increase format diversity:
- Text
- Infographics
- Carousels
- Short videos

But keep:
- Same core narrative
- Same tone
- Same positioning

People should recognize your voice instantly.

### Step 3: Collaborate with other creators

At this stage, collabs become realistic and highly effective.

How:
- Engage with creators in your niche first
- Identify 5 creators with overlapping topics
- Propose practical co-created posts (especially carousels)

Collabs create:
- Audience crossover
- Social proof
- Faster credibility growth

### Step 4: Develop a signature format

Top creators become recognizable through a repeatable format.

Your signature could be:
- A visual style
- A storytelling structure
- A framework pattern
- A recurring type of breakdown or experiment

When your format is recognizable, your profile picture becomes the hook.

Then stay consistent long enough for compounding to take over.

## Full 0 to 10K recap

Phase 1 (0 to 2K):

- 10x engagement over posting

Phase 2 (2K to 5K):
- Double down on winners
- Introduce infographics

Phase 3 (5K to 10K):
- Install repurposing system
- Multiply formats

This is the exact playbook Pierre would use to rebuild from zero to 10,000 LinkedIn followers.

## Summary

Pierre's framework positions LinkedIn growth as a staged operating system, not a viral lottery. Early growth depends on sharp positioning and profile conversion; mid-stage growth comes from pattern recognition and authority-building formats; late-stage growth depends on systems, collaboration, and brand recognizability. The central idea is compounding: each phase builds assets (clarity, trust, repeatable formats, and relationships) that make subsequent growth easier and less dependent on daily creative effort.

## Application to LinkedIn for B2B SaaS

1. Start with ICP precision
- Define one buyer type, one pain, one offer before scaling content.

2. Optimize profile as conversion surface
- Ensure headline, banner, featured proof, and CTA all match content promise.

3. Use authority cadences early
- Run a 30-day sprint with 3 repeatable topic pillars tied to buyer pain.

4. Build a comments-first distribution loop
- For small accounts, strategic commenting can outperform early posting reach.

5. Standardize post analysis
- Every 2-4 weeks, identify top hooks/topics and create "v2/v3" variants.

6. Move from creator to media system
- Shift to repurposing workflows (1 idea -> multi-format outputs).

7. Build defensible attention assets
- Creator partnerships + signature format + consistent voice = stronger long-term moat.
