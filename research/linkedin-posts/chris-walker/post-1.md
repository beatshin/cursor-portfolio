Post Content:
Every category-creating company gets called a scam before it gets called a category.

Airbnb was dismissed as a liability nightmare.
Uber was called illegal and unsafe.
Salesforce was called technically naive and commercially unviable.
Peloton was called overpriced and gimmicky.

The critics were never the customers.
The loudest people saying something will not work are often people who never used it, or who lose if it succeeds.

Chris then shares two personal examples:
- Refine Labs: he challenged the MQL model and faced criticism before the view became mainstream in B2B marketing.
- ENCODED: he received harsh skepticism from non-users while sharing strong customer-reported outcomes.

Core claim:
Market validation should come from real customers who used the product, not uninformed social media criticism.

Closing message:
If you're building something new and meaningful, criticism is expected.
Block out noise, stay focused on customers, and stay true to your vision.
