Post Content:
Two years ago after our first event - late night we joked "did we just become an events business?"

After dragging my heels on becoming an events business - because of the costs, the travel, the stress.

It's one thing if people don't show up to your webinar or no one listens to your podcast, but to deal with people getting plane tickets, flying across the country, and getting their butts in seats for your event, man that is a different kind of stress.

But we have ABSOLUTELY become an events business because in-person is an INCREDIBLE force multiplier for community.

Because we have this online community (Exit Five), some percentage of event attendees feel like they already know each other, and even if that's only 25% of the room it makes a big, big difference.

I am sure you saw the posts from attendees on LinkedIn over the last two weeks.
That is the type of PR and brand you can't pay for.
It's genuine. It's real.

As Sydney Sloan put it:
"COMMUNITY EVENTS FEEL DIFFERENT... It's not networking, it's a reunion of people who actually care about each other."

And the way I spin the story about IRL is that it's more important now than ever, because we're all spending so much time working with AI, we can't forget how great real human connection feels.

I've been told this is a very American thing, but we put a lot of stock into what we do for work.
Our jobs are a big part of our identity.
And there's something about millions of people on this earth who work in B2B marketing.
I'm honored that we've built something with Exit Five where we can sit at the middle of connecting those people.

You! You people.

I have LOTS of other event lessons and learnings, but I'm over the stage of sharing them now because we have something special and it's a secret sauce I'm keeping close.

Granted, I didn't do anything for this event other than MC and post about it to all of my LinkedIn followers - but that's the power of building a great team at E5.

Worth every penny of payroll going all in on this company after telling everyone I could only be solopreneur for life.

We have something special.
"Who cares it's just B2B marketing bro."
Nah you don't get it then :)
