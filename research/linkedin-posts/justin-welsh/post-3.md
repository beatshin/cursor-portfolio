I always thought bold people were just built differently than I am.

I don't believe that anymore.

Not after a dinner with my friend Steve last night.

Back in 2021, Steve made one of the boldest moves I've seen.
He emailed 50+ investors he'd just raised millions from, told them he was shutting the fund down, and returned the money.

My hands were literally sweating just listening to him tell the story.

But it turns out, Steve had the same fears you or I would have.
He'd just quietly done the prep work for years.

- He ran tiny experiments outside of VC.
- He had proof he'd be okay financially.
- He hit send while feeling "scared shitless."

Those are the things we all don't notice.

We envy the final "bold" move but miss the fear, the prep, and the runway people have built to make it all possible.

I've done versions of this myself.

I walked away from a $70M ARR company to write online.
I shut down two revenue streams while they were still making 75% of my income.
We moved to a 552-person hamlet in the Hudson Valley and decided that was home.

Each decision felt scary when I made it, but I'd been quietly preparing for years.

That preparation is what buys you the life afterward:
- Quieter mornings
- A lighter calendar
- Work you actually want to do
- Days you intentionally design yourself

I spend a lot of my time thinking about these types of choices.
