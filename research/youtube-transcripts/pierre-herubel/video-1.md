Cleaned Transcript:

We are in 2026, and in my opinion, LinkedIn content strategy needs a total revamp.

In this video, I explain how I built an audience of over 160,000 marketers and founders around the world. If you want to build a real community, you need:
- A specific topic
- A clear strategy for how you talk about that topic
- Fresh, unique content that stands out
- A distribution strategy (what to post, when to post, and how to engage)

Most creators still use the TOFU-MOFU-BOFU framework:
- TOFU (top of funnel): broad, relatable stories
- MOFU (middle of funnel): broad expertise content
- BOFU (bottom of funnel): sales content

The logic is: attract broadly, refine with authority, then sell.

I believe this model is often not ideal for niche B2B:
1. If your business is specific, broad audiences are usually unqualified.
2. If you attract the wrong followers, they will not engage when you post about your actual offer.
3. You become addicted to broad content because it gets likes, even if it does not convert.
4. LinkedIn increasingly rewards expert content over generic personal storytelling.

My alternative is the Authority-First Framework.

Over the past four years, this framework helped me generate 50M+ impressions and 160K+ followers. It looks similar on paper, but the mindset is different: lead with expertise first.

## 1) Authority Content (70%)

Most of your content should be industry expertise:
- Opinions on your field
- Analysis and trends
- How-tos and practical breakdowns
- Insights from your own experience

The goal is not just audience growth; the goal is authority.

Your content must be original. Do not copy competitors or rely on generic AI outputs. Share insights that come from your real expertise so your content cannot be commoditized.

## 2) Personal Story Content (15%)

Once authority is established, add personal content tied to your professional journey:
- How you got where you are
- Behind-the-scenes of your work
- Build-in-public updates
- Personal opinions connected to your niche

People buy from people. Personal content helps your audience connect with you, trust you, and remember you.

When I added this after building authority, I saw an uplift in inbound leads and close rates.

## 3) Offer / Conversion Content (15%)

Use this content to convert authority into pipeline and revenue.

First, make sure your profile and offer are clear, coherent, and high-value.

Then choose a concrete objective per post:
- Newsletter signups
- Product users
- Free trials
- Demo/call bookings
- Coaching clients

Use the PAS framework:
- Problem: define the problem with a real example
- Agitate: show why the pain is costly and urgent
- Solution: present your offer and include a CTA

This turns content into conversion.

## Authority-First Mix Recap

- 70% Authority content (expertise, trends, analysis, opinions)
- 15% Personal content (stories, behind-the-scenes, founder journey)
- 15% Offer content (PAS + clear CTA)

This framework is not ideal if:
- You only care about broad follower growth
- You do not want to be seen as an expert
- You have nothing to sell

But if you want a qualified audience, stronger expert positioning, and real business outcomes, this is a strong strategy for LinkedIn in 2026.

## Summary

Pierre argues that the classic TOFU-MOFU-BOFU model can underperform for niche B2B because broad reach often attracts unqualified audiences. His Authority-First Framework prioritizes expert content first (70%), then personal content (15%), then offer content (15%). The core idea is to build trust through original, experience-based expertise before asking for conversion. Personal posts humanize the brand once authority is established, and offer posts should use clear conversion goals and direct CTAs.

## Application to LinkedIn for B2B SaaS

For B2B SaaS, treat LinkedIn as a category education and demand capture channel, not just a vanity metrics channel.

1. Define a narrow authority lane
- Pick 1-2 topics tied to your product value (for example: RevOps automation, SOC2 workflow, onboarding analytics).
- Anchor content to ICP pain points, buying triggers, and implementation friction.

2. Run a weekly 70/15/15 content cadence
- Authority (70%): publish tactical breakdowns, benchmark takes, implementation mistakes, and POV posts on market shifts.
- Personal (15%): share founder/operator lessons, product-building decisions, and behind-the-scenes tradeoffs.
- Offer (15%): post direct conversion content for trials, demos, webinars, or lead magnets.

3. Convert expertise into pipeline
- Use PAS in offer posts with concrete business pain (lost revenue, churn risk, wasted headcount hours).
- Add one CTA per post (book demo, start trial, download checklist), and align it with a dedicated landing page.

4. Optimize for qualified demand, not just engagement
- Track qualified profile views, ICP comments, demo requests, and opportunity creation from LinkedIn.
- Reduce generic broad posts if they inflate likes but do not produce sales conversations.

5. Build a simple operating system
- Create a repeatable content bank from sales calls, onboarding calls, customer objections, and product release notes.
- Have founders/experts own authority posts; repurpose strong posts into carousels, short videos, and email content.

Example monthly execution for a B2B SaaS team (12 posts):
- 8 authority posts
- 2 personal/founder posts
- 2 offer/conversion posts

This keeps positioning strong while steadily turning attention into qualified pipeline.
