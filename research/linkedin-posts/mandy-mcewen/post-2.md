Post Content:
Most LinkedIn headlines are completely useless.

"Empowering efficiency for SaaS companies."
What does that even mean? Seriously.

If a prospect reads your profile and still has to guess what you do, you already lost.
People don't want clever. They want clear.

Your profile should answer two things instantly:
-> Who you help
-> How you help them

That's it.

Most people stay vague because they're thinking about how they sound.
Not how buyers think.

Buyers are scanning.
They're asking themselves one question:
"Can this person help me?"

So compare these two:
"Empowering efficiency for SaaS companies."
vs.
"Helping sales reps book 30% more meetings."

One is vague.
One tells you the exact outcome.
Clarity beats clever every single time.

If your LinkedIn headline feels "fancy" but people still ask what you do, it's time to rewrite it.
Or maybe it just says your job title.

So here's the test.
Could a stranger understand exactly what you do in 5 seconds?
